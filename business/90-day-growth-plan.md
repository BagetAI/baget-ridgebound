# Ridgebound 90-Day Business & Growth Plan (2026)

**TL;DR**
Ridgebound captures the "Logistics Gap" for elite solo hikers by automating 40+ hours of administrative friction. We will reach $15k MRR by October 2026 by securing 6 Expedition Partner clients and 5 Concierge clients per month, driven by high-authority technical content on "unsolvable" routes like the Hayduke and Brooks Range.

---

## 1. Financial Path to $15k MRR

To achieve a sustainable $15,000 monthly revenue target, Ridgebound will focus on the high-margin "Expedition" and "Concierge" segments. The "Scout" tier serves as a low-friction entry point to build the email list and trust.

| Tier | Price | Target Monthly Volume | Monthly Revenue |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **The Scout** | $499 | 1 clients | $499 |
| **The Concierge** | $999 | 5 clients | $4,995 |
| **The Expedition Partner** | $2,499 | 4 clients | $9,996 |
| **Total** | | **10 clients** | **$15,490** |

### Margin Optimization
*   **Asset-Light Model**: 0% ownership of vehicles or aircraft. Ridgebound takes a 20-30% management fee on top of pass-through costs (shuttles, bush planes).
*   **Contractor Network**: Use a "Preferred Partner" program to negotiate volume discounts with bush plane operators in Bettles and shuttle drivers in Escalante.

---

## 2. Expert Content Strategy: The Authority Flywheel

We will acquire the first 10 clients by solving the "Information Asymmetry" problem. Our content will not be "inspirational"; it will be "technical and actionable," targeting the exact friction points that stop hikers from attempting remote routes.

### Target Content Pillars
1.  **The 2026 Hayduke Water Report**: A monthly updated deep-dive on cache site accessibility and dirt road conditions.
2.  **Brooks Range Logistics 101**: A cost breakdown of Coyote Air vs. Wright Air Service for 2026, including weight limit hacks for resupply drops.
3.  **The Permit Lottery Script**: A data-backed guide on the exact minute and second to click "Book" on Recreation.gov for the Sierra High Route (SEKI).

### Channel Distribution
*   **High-Intent SEO**: Target keywords: "Hayduke water cache service," "Brooks Range bush plane cost," "Sierra High Route permit lottery strategy."
*   **Community Infiltration**: Active, technical participation in the "Hayduke Trail" and "Long Distance Hiking" Facebook groups and Reddit r/Ultralight. 
*   **Partnerships**: Outreach to gear manufacturers (e.g., Zpacks, Hyperlite Mountain Gear) to provide "Logistics Guides" for their customers.

## 3. 90-Day Execution Roadmap (May - July 2026)

### Phase 1: Foundation & Authority (Days 1-30)
*   **Digital Hub**: Deploy a high-performance landing page with technical route guides for the Top 3 high-friction routes (Brooks, Hayduke, Sierra).
*   **Stripe Integration**: Connect the three pricing tiers for immediate checkout.
*   **The "Logistics Audit" Lead Magnet**: Offer a free 15-minute "Permit Strategy Call" to capture high-intent emails.

### Phase 2: Acquisition & Partnership (Days 31-60)
*   **Vendor Lock-in**: Sign formal MOUs with 5 key logistics partners (e.g., Escalante Shuttles, FlyCoyote).
*   **Expert Outreach**: Send 50 personalized pitches to hikers who have expressed interest in remote routes on social platforms.
*   **Paid Search**: Execute a $1k/month Google Ads campaign targeting "Sierra High Route permits" and "Glacier NP backcountry lottery."

### Phase 3: Scale & Fulfillment (Days 61-90)
*   **Client Intake**: Onboard first 10 "Concierge" and "Expedition" clients for the late 2026/early 2027 season.
*   **SOP Automation**: Implement a CRM to track permit deadlines and resupply shipping windows automatically.
*   **Referral Flywheel**: Launch the "Trail Partner" referral program (10% credit for every client referred).

## 4. Operational Risk Management

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
|:---|:---|
| **Permit Lottery Failure** | Guarantee "Plan B" route development (e.g., Wind River High Route as a backup for Sierra). |
| **Vendor Cancellation** | Maintain a "Secondary Operator" list for all flight and shuttle legs. |
| **Emergency SOS** | All Expedition clients must carry a Garmin inReach synced to our 24/7 monitoring dashboard. |
| **Regulatory Change** | Quartery audits of NPS/BLM commercial use authorizations (CUA). |
